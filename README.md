**Background**

Ballroom dance students accumulate a significant volume of lesson activity over time — spanning multiple instructors, dance styles, lesson types, and skill-building notes. This information is typically captured informally in spreadsheets or notes, making it difficult to review progress, identify patterns, or prioritize what to work on next.

-----

**The Problem**

A dedicated ballroom dance student taking private lessons, coaching sessions, and practice rounds across multiple instructors and dance styles has no structured way to:

Track lesson history in a consistent, searchable format
Identify high-priority items that need revisiting before they are forgotten
Analyze progress patterns — which dances, instructors, and lesson types are receiving the most (or least) attention
Organize coaching insights separately from regular lesson notes, since coaching lessons carry distinct value as milestone feedback
Reference a living catalog of dance figures organized by style and type

As a result, valuable lesson notes go unreviewed, coaching feedback fades, and opportunities to focus practice intentionally are missed.

-----

**The Solution**

The Dance Lesson Tracker App is a personal, browser-based tool built with Streamlit that gives a dance student a single place to:

Log and browse every lesson — filterable by date, instructor, dance style, dance type, lesson type, and priority
Surface what matters most — a High Priority view highlights lessons explicitly flagged for follow-up
Understand their training profile — charts and breakdowns reveal where time and coaching investment are concentrated
Track coaching history — a dedicated view isolates lessons with guest coaches, capturing who taught what and when
Build a dance figure reference — editable tables organized by Smooth and Rhythm styles serve as a growing personal syllabus

-----

**The Target Audience**

A single dance student managing their own training log — someone who values intentional practice and wants their lesson data to inform how they train, not just record that they trained.
